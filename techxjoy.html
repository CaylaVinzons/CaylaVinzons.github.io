Technology x Joy Prototypes
August - December 2016
Atlanta, GA

I joined a studio at the Adaptive Media Lab at Georgia Tech which plans to exhibit art installations with the theme “Technology and Joy.” Over the course of 3 ½ months, we created, ideated, and prototyped a series of installation ideas. We worked in conjunction with the Eyedrum Gallery, with some of the residents there.

The first installation idea was a VR experience for the HTC Vive. The player began in a desolate forest, and through natural gestures, could restore light and color to the area. Our prototype experimented with a few gestures and a custom shader, but the idea was to have a large map, where visitors could keep coming back to the forest and check on the status of its restoration.

The second installation idea was a storytelling experience entitled The Confabulator. The confabulator was a device that would play on a question we ask when we tell stories: What if? What if something happened different, or I ran into someone else? For our prototype, we used RiTa.js, ConceptNet5, and CMUSphnix. We allowed users to voice input a story, and then we sorted the user’s words by part of speech and re-told the story in a fairy-tale context, using a grammar we created from RiTa.
